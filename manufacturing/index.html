Case Study · Manufacturing

React-to-Salesforce Integration for a Global Steel Manufacturer

A developer-led partnership that integrated a custom React frontend with Salesforce and modernized legacy Apex across two enterprise divisions.

Global Steel Manufacturer · $12.9B ARR · 16,500 Employees

About the Client

A global steel manufacturer operating multiple brands across continents, $12.9B ARR and roughly 16,500 employees, undergoing a major digital modernization.

Challenge

One division needed to integrate a custom-built React frontend with Salesforce, but their internal team lacked the capacity and deep technical expertise to handle the complexity. The architecture involved legacy Apex code, rigid third-party systems, and inconsistent data mapping across currencies and systems. After that initiative, a second brand within the enterprise brought Free Thinkers Consulting in to support their internal CRM, a critical but aging piece of their operational infrastructure.

Solution

To support the custom React application, Free Thinkers Consulting engineered a robust REST-based integration layer between the frontend and Salesforce. This included refactoring legacy Apex code, building scalable REST services, and enabling secure, real-time data exchange across platforms.

Once the initial integration initiative concluded, a second division engaged Free Thinkers Consulting to support their internal CRM, a legacy Salesforce org with heavy technical debt and deep connections to rigid third-party systems. Acting as an embedded development partner, Free Thinkers provided strategic enhancements, stabilized aging code, and delivered custom logic to bridge data mismatches, multi-currency flows, and limited API capabilities from external tools.

Results

  • Seamless integration between React frontend and Salesforce via custom REST APIs
  • Legacy Apex refactored to support maintainability, performance, and reusability
  • Reduced internal technical debt across two enterprise divisions
  • Ongoing issue resolution and enhancements across rigid third-party system boundaries
  • Multi-currency and data mapping inconsistencies resolved via custom middleware logic
  • A trusted developer-led partnership supporting long-term Salesforce strategy
